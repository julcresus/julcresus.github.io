Wonderbly 2025 – present
One product format. Three different audiences. Consistent voice.

Wonderbly's newspaper books are built from real historical archives and personalised by the customer. The football book speaks to tribal identity and lifelong passion. The American football book uses the same template but different cultural cues. The family history book is a different emotional register entirely — nostalgia, legacy, generations. As Producer, I oversee the editorial management and tech build of these books, brief the marketing team on copy, help shape the customer creation journey, and pitch new titles. The content challenge is maintaining a recognisable product voice while adapting to audiences that feel completely different from each other.

Football
"It's more than just their club's story — it's part of who they are."
Family
"A gift that brings generations together."
Same product format, same archive, same structure — but the copy has to work for a completely different emotional need. Identity and belonging for the sports fan; legacy and connection for the family book. Getting that distinction right is a content decision, not a marketing one.

Trigger Publishing & Cherish Editions 2021 – 2025
Plain language in high-stakes content

Over four years at Trigger and Cherish Editions — as Editor then Managing Editor — I edited 12+ titles per season across mental health and children's books, wrote back-cover blurbs and cover design briefs, drafted press releases, managed metadata and distribution, and occasionally commissioned editors. The content challenge wasn't just accuracy: it was making difficult material feel safe, readable, and genuinely useful to someone who might be in crisis.

Before
"Patients presenting with anhedonia and avolition may benefit from behavioural activation protocols."
After
"If you've lost interest in things you used to enjoy, small structured activities can help — even when it doesn't feel like it."
Clinical terms removed without losing meaning. The rewrite shifts from third-person diagnostic to direct second-person — addressing the reader in distress, not a clinician.
Process
[ Replace with your real process — e.g.: ] I flagged passages where clinical language created distance from the reader, then checked each proposed change against Trigger's editorial guidelines and the Samaritans' media guidance. Where I removed a clinical term, I made sure the plain-language version preserved the original meaning exactly — accuracy and accessibility aren't a trade-off, they're both required.

Four years editing in this space gave me a deep instinct for content that serves readers in vulnerable moments — not just what's correct, but what's genuinely useful.

About

I'm a content designer with a decade of editorial experience. I currently work at Wonderbly as a Producer, overseeing the content and design of personalised books that reach families all over the world.

Before that, I spent four years at Trigger Publishing and Cherish Editions — first as Editor, then Managing Editor — working on mental health and wellbeing books. That work shaped how I think about content: the reader might be in crisis, they might be confused, they might be skeptical. Your job is to meet them where they are.

I hold an MA in Magazine Journalism from Kingston University (Postgraduate Student of the Year, NCTJ Gold Standard with Distinction) and a BA in Journalism and Advertising from UNC Chapel Hill. Before moving to London I spent three years teaching English in Madrid — which is where the Spanish comes from, and where I learned that clarity across languages is a skill, not just a nicety.

I'm looking for content design roles where editorial depth matters — in health, wellbeing, or consumer products. I bring rigour, care, and a genuine belief that the right words in the right place can make someone's day meaningfully better.
